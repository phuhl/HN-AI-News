  - title: Inference cost at scale with napkin math
    link: https://injuly.in/blog/napkin-inference-cost/index.html
    domain: injuly.in
    summary: Napkin math shows a single Nvidia B200 GPU can serve 300-800 chat users on a 32B model for roughly $9/user/month
    points: 66
    hn_url: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48560227
    comments: 0
    time: Jun 20, 20:50 UTC
    content_bullets:
    - A B200's 8 TB/s memory bandwidth — not its 4500 TFLOP/s compute — is the binding constraint for LLM inference, leaving
      compute cores idle ~98% of the time.
    - KV-cache lets each forward pass process only the newest token instead of the full history, cutting memory accesses to
      ~2×B operations and enabling ~40 tokens/sec per user.
    - With Grouped-Query-Attention and PagedAttention, one B200 can sustain 40-60 concurrent users at 100% duty cycle, or
      300-800 users under realistic chat-app idle patterns.
    - Buying a B200 outright (~$40k) yields a lifetime per-user hardware cost of ~$133 at 300 users; renting at $4/hr translates
      to roughly $9.36/user/month.
    - The analysis uses a 32B dense model as the reference; the author notes calculations are conservative and leave headroom
      for heavier workloads.
    discussion_bullets:
    - Commenters flag that real frontier models like DeepSeek use MoE (284B total / 13B activated), which changes memory and
      batching dynamics significantly and may invalidate the 32B dense baseline.
    - A commenter's quick counter-calculation adds electricity cost for a B200 (~600W average, $0.14/kWh) to the rental math,
      arriving at only ~$0.20/user/month for power — a small but non-trivial addition once datacenter PUE, cooling, and maintenance
      are included.
    - Others note that 32B activated on an MoE model is roughly equivalent in inference cost to a 32B dense model, partially
      defending the article's approach despite the architectural mismatch.
