    - Hallucinated claims on high-stakes medical/legal/financial prompts dropped 52.5%

      Gmail data shaped a reply; rolling out to Plus/Pro web users first.

      from the full GPT-5.5, causing naming confusion since the Instant variant is less
      capable than the full release.
    - The 52.5% hallucination reduction claim drew skepticism about whether internal
      benchmarks hold up in everyday real-world use outside curated test sets.
    - The memory-sources transparency panel was broadly welcomed as an overdue feature,
      giving users visibility and control over personalization context for the first time.

      reasoning.

      token, prompting some developers to switch coding pipelines despite weaker
