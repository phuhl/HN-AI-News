        content_bullets:
          - "LeRobot's gRPC PolicyServer passes raw data through Python's pickle.loads() across RPC endpoints without validation — a classic deserialization footgun that permits arbitrary code execution."
          - "Configured with add_insecure_port() — no TLS, no authentication — so any network-reachable attacker can deliver a malicious pickle payload and execute arbitrary system commands."
          - "Default binding is localhost, but production deployments commonly expose the service on 0.0.0.0; that gap earns the flaw a CVSS score of 9.8 and is how it becomes remotely exploitable."
          - "No patch exists yet; a fix is planned for version 0.6.0. Interim mitigations: replace pickle with safetensors, JSON, or protobuf, and add TLS plus gRPC authentication."
          - "In robotics environments LeRobot backs physical hardware; a successful exploit can cause hardware disruption, raising the stakes beyond typical remote-code-execution incidents."
