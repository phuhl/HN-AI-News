  - title: What Are Tokens in LLMs?
    link: https://bearisland.dev/posts/tokens-and-tokenization/
    domain: bearisland.dev
    summary: A clear primer on how LLM tokenization works — explaining BPE, vocabulary trade-offs, and why models can't count letters in 'strawberry'
    points: 10
    hn_url: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48438276
    comments: 6
    time: Jun 7, 21:04 UTC
    content_bullets:
    - Tokens are the smallest input unit a model perceives; the same text tokenizes differently across models (e.g., 9 tokens in GPT-4 vs. 7 in Llama 3 for one phrase).
    - Byte Pair Encoding (BPE) is pure frequency-based merging — no neural network involved — iteratively combining the most common adjacent pairs until a target vocabulary size is reached.
    - Modern tokenizers start from 256 byte values (not characters), ensuring every input is representable and eliminating out-of-vocabulary failures, but at higher token cost for non-Latin scripts.
    - 'Vocabulary size (30K–256K tokens) involves trade-offs: larger vocabularies compress text but inflate embedding matrices and hurt rare tokens with few training updates.'
    - 'The ''strawberry'' letter-counting failure is architectural: GPT-4 splits it as ''str''+''aw''+''berry'', so the model never sees individual characters and must reason around its own blindspot.'
    discussion_bullets:
    - 'A top commenter (Tiberium) reinforces the core insight: models don''t fail at counting letters due to stupidity — they simply never see letters at all, only integer indices into a private vocabulary.'
    - The thread highlights that tokenizer vocabularies are model-specific (GPT-4 ≠ Claude ≠ Llama), making tokenization a hidden source of cross-model behavioral differences.
