  - title: Jensen–Shannon Divergence
    link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jensen%E2%80%93Shannon_divergence
    domain: en.wikipedia.org
    summary: 'Jensen-Shannon Divergence: The Symmetric Distance Metric That Powers GAN Training and Language Model Evaluation'
    points: 89
    hn_url: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48240356
    comments: 14
    time: May 25, 20:18 UTC
    content_bullets:
    - JSD measures how different two probability distributions are by comparing each to their average — unlike KL divergence,
      it works symmetrically and always gives a finite answer.
    - Its square root is a true mathematical distance (metric), making it usable in clustering and nearest-neighbor algorithms
      where KL divergence would fail.
    - 'Bounded between 0 and 1 (with log base 2), JSD is easy to interpret: 0 means identical distributions, 1 means completely
      non-overlapping.'
    - JSD underpins GAN training theory — the original GAN paper showed the generator minimizes JSD between real and generated
      data distributions.
    - Used in NLP and language model evaluation to compare token/word distributions, and in bioinformatics for genome and
      protein surface comparison.
    discussion_bullets:
    - Comments likely explored the practical tradeoffs between JSD and KL divergence, noting KL's asymmetry causes issues
      when distributions have non-overlapping support (a common GAN training problem JSD avoids).
    - The metric property of sqrt(JSD) drew interest — this makes it geometrically meaningful in ways raw divergences are
      not, enabling use in spaces where triangle inequality must hold.
    - Discussion probably touched on connections to information theory (Shannon entropy) and why JSD is sometimes preferred
      over Wasserstein distance in certain generative model contexts.
